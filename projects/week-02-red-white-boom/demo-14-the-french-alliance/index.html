FEBRUARY 6, 1778 · HÔTEL DE COISLIN, PARIS
France enters the war. The Revolution has its lifeline.
Historical Note The Treaty of Alliance and Treaty of Amity and Commerce were signed February 6, 1778. France recognized American independence and formally entered the war against Britain. The French fleet and 6,000 troops under Rochambeau proved decisive at Yorktown in 1781, forcing Cornwallis's surrender and effectively ending the Revolution. Benjamin Franklin negotiated from Paris until 1785.

Historical Note In reality, Franklin succeeded brilliantly — aided by the stunning American victory at Saratoga (October 1777), which convinced Vergennes that America could actually win. Without French intervention, the Continental Army faced insurmountable odds: a superior Royal Navy, professional Hessian troops, and dwindling colonial support. Most historians consider the French Alliance the single most decisive factor in American independence.
